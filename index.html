Stress & Anxiety Relief: Mindfulness, Yoga & Meditation

Mindfulness Exercises

Body Scan Meditation

This mindfulness exercise helps you tune into your body and can help reduce anxiety and stress by promoting deep relaxation.

  • Find a quiet space and lie down comfortably.
  • Focus on your breath and slowly scan your body from feet to head.
  • Release any tension and breathe deeply.

Mindful Breathing

Focus your attention on your breath to reduce anxiety and calm your nervous system.

  • Sit in a comfortable position and close your eyes.
  • Breathe deeply, counting to 4 on the inhale, hold for 4, and exhale for 6.
  • Focus on your breath and gently bring your attention back when it wanders.

5-4-3-2-1 Grounding Exercise

This exercise helps you stay present and reconnect with the world around you when feeling overwhelmed.

  • Find a comfortable seated position and take a few deep breaths.
  • Notice 5 things you can see, 4 things you can touch, 3 things you can hear, 2 things you can smell, and 1 thing you can taste.
  • This simple exercise can help you stay grounded and reduce anxiety.

Breathing with Counting

This exercise helps calm the mind by focusing on controlled breathing.

  • Find a quiet, comfortable position to sit.
  • Inhale deeply for a count of 4, hold for 4, and exhale slowly for a count of 6.
  • Repeat for 5-10 minutes, keeping your attention on the counting and breath.

Yoga for Stress & Anxiety Relief

Child's Pose

This gentle stretch helps relieve tension in the back, shoulders, and neck, promoting relaxation.

  • Start on hands and knees, then sit back onto your heels.
  • Extend your arms forward, relax your forehead on the floor.
  • Stay here for 1-3 minutes, breathing deeply.

Downward-Facing Dog

This pose stretches the entire body, especially the back and legs, while calming the mind.

  • Start in a tabletop position, then lift your hips up and back.
  • Press your heels towards the floor and relax your head between your arms.
  • Hold the pose for 1-3 minutes, focusing on your breath.

Legs Up the Wall

This restorative pose helps calm the nervous system and relieve stress by promoting circulation.

  • Find a wall and sit with one side of your body against it.
  • Swing your legs up the wall, and lie down with your back on the floor.
  • Rest here for 5-15 minutes, focusing on your breath.

Seated Forward Fold

This pose helps stretch the back and hamstrings while calming the mind.

  • Sit on the floor with your legs extended straight in front of you.
  • Slowly hinge at the hips, bringing your chest towards your thighs.
  • Hold the position for 1-3 minutes, breathing deeply and relaxing your upper body.

Meditation for Stress & Anxiety Relief

Guided Meditation

This meditation practice helps to calm your mind and ease anxiety through deep breathing and visualization.

  • Sit comfortably and close your eyes.
  • Focus on your breath, visualizing a peaceful place.
  • Release anxious thoughts and stay in the present moment.

Loving-Kindness Meditation

This practice fosters compassion for yourself and others, promoting feelings of peace and connection.

  • Focus on yourself, then others, wishing them happiness and peace.
  • Extend these well-wishes to all beings in the world.
  • Stay in the moment and observe feelings of compassion.
